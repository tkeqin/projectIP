# Self-Assessment Module - Quick Start Guide

## What is the Self-Assessment Module?
The Self-Assessment Module is a mental health self-evaluation tool for students at MindWell. It allows students to take quick assessments about their mood, stress, anxiety, and general wellbeing, and receive personalized feedback and recommendations.

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Access the Module
1. Log in to your MindWell account as a student
2. Go to the Student Home page
3. Click the **"Take Assessment"** button in Quick Actions
4. Alternatively, navigate directly to: `/student/assessment/`

### Step 2: Choose an Assessment
Select one of the four available assessments:

| Assessment | Duration | Focus |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Daily Mood Check | 2 min | Emotional state and patterns |
| Stress Assessment | 5 min | Stress levels and triggers |
| Anxiety Screening | 7 min | Anxiety symptoms and severity |
| General Wellbeing | 10 min | Overall mental wellness |

### Step 3: Complete the Assessment
- **Answer each question** by selecting one of five options
- **Use Previous/Next buttons** to navigate between questions
- **You can use arrow keys** on your keyboard for quick navigation
- **All responses are private and secure**

### Step 4: Review Your Results
After submitting, you'll see:
- **Your Overall Score** (0-25 scale)
- **Score Category** (Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor)
- **Personalized Feedback** tailored to your responses
- **Wellness Metrics** showing your engagement, consistency, and progress
- **Recommended Actions** specific to your assessment type

### Step 5: Take Action
- Follow the recommended actions
- Take another assessment to track progress
- Return to your dashboard to explore other resources

## Understanding Your Results

### Score Categories

**Excellent (70-100)**
- Your wellness is in excellent condition
- Continue with your current practices
- Explore advanced coping techniques

**Good (50-69)**
- Your wellness is good overall
- Small improvements could enhance your experience
- Consider exploring new wellness strategies

**Fair (30-49)**
- Your wellness needs attention
- Consider implementing suggested strategies
- Reach out for support when needed

**Poor (0-29)**
- Your wellness requires immediate attention
- Professional support is recommended
- Please contact a counselor for personalized guidance

### Wellness Metrics

**Engagement**: Shows your level of participation and involvement in wellness activities

**Consistency**: Indicates how consistently you maintain wellness practices

**Progress**: Demonstrates your progress in your wellness journey

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be Honest**: Your responses are only used to help you - be truthful for accurate feedback
2. **Take Time**: Don't rush through answers - think carefully about each question
3. **Peaceful Environment**: Complete assessments in a calm, distraction-free space
4. **Regular Check-ins**: Take assessments regularly (weekly recommended) to track progress
5. **Act on Recommendations**: Follow the suggested actions to see real improvements

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are my responses private?**
A: Yes, all responses are completely private and secure. Only you and counselors (if you share) can see your results.

**Q: Can I retake an assessment?**
A: Yes! You can take the same assessment multiple times. Try taking it weekly to track trends.

**Q: What if I get a poor score?**
A: A poor score indicates you might benefit from professional support. Please reach out to:
- Your campus counselor
- Mental health resources listed in your dashboard
- The AI chatbot for immediate support

**Q: How long does an assessment take?**
A: - Daily Mood Check: ~2 minutes
- Stress Assessment: ~5 minutes
- Anxiety Screening: ~7 minutes
- General Wellbeing: ~10 minutes

**Q: Can I save my results?**
A: Results are saved in your current session. For permanent records, consider discussing results with a counselor.

**Q: What should I do with my recommendations?**
A: - Read through them carefully
- Pick one or two to start with
- Integrate them into your daily routine
- Track changes in subsequent assessments

**Q: Is this a replacement for professional help?**
A: No. This tool is for self-assessment and awareness. If you're struggling significantly, please seek professional help from a counselor or mental health provider.

## Assessment Types Overview

### Daily Mood Check
**Best for:** Quick emotional check-ins
**Questions cover:**
1. How would you describe your current mood?
2. What is your energy level today?
3. How connected do you feel to others?
4. How well did you sleep last night?
5. How satisfied are you with your overall well-being?

### Stress Assessment
**Best for:** Understanding stress levels and coping
**Questions cover:**
1. How stressed are you feeling right now?
2. How often do you feel overwhelmed?
3. How well do you manage stress?
4. How does stress affect your daily life?
5. Do you have support for managing stress?

### Anxiety Screening
**Best for:** Understanding anxiety symptoms
**Questions cover:**
1. How often do you feel anxious?
2. Do you experience physical symptoms?
3. How much do anxious thoughts interfere?
4. Can you control your anxiety?
5. How would you rate your anxiety severity?

### General Wellbeing
**Best for:** Comprehensive wellness assessment
**Questions cover:**
1. How is your physical health?
2. How is your mental health?
3. How satisfied are you with your life?
4. Do you feel a sense of purpose?
5. How are your relationships?

## Recommended Assessment Schedule

- **Weekly**: Daily Mood Check (quick pulse check)
- **Bi-weekly**: Stress Assessment (track stress patterns)
- **Monthly**: Anxiety Screening & General Wellbeing (comprehensive check)

In this version of MindWell:

- Data is not permanently saved
- No reports are sent to counselors automatically
- You can share results if you choose
